Smart Retail Checkout (Proof of concept)

This was an original idea born out of the thought of how easy I wished my shopping experience would be. To be able to purchase items from a supermarket without having to queue for long periods in order to checkout. I also had the streneous task of offloading from cart or basket and repackaging into carrier bags in mind. Both of which consume alot of time that if saved would make the shopping experience much more pleasant.

A survey also shows that retail shops lose millions in sales due to abandoned carts. [Source:Kwak, J. K. (2017). Analysis On The Effect Of Express Checkouts In Retail Stores. Journal of Applied Business Research, 33(4), 767]. This survey was conducted in Europe but it speaks to human behaviour. This means the same phenomenon is experienced across the globe.

This implementation may not be practical at the moment considering the costs, but technology is advancing at a fast rate and if this model is improved over time and the technology also becomes more affordable, the entire model will be cost friendly and practical.

The general idea is to be able to walk into a supermarket, pick items, accept payment prompt on mobile device and walk out.

I use facial recognition to identify the shopper and RFID technology to identify the picked item. I then relay the billing information to the shopper's mobile application and await payment. The payment gateway used in this project is a local mobile payment platform known as M-PESA.

A question of security arises. The same technology can be used to link pending payments to individuals identified at exit.
